Wersja oryginalna:
Large language models are tools with great potential for text processing. The present study aims to assess the reliability of the models' results in extracting structured knowledge from unstructured textual sources, particularly biographies from the Polish Biographical Dictionary (PSB). The task of the model was to extract information about the individuals such as date and place of death, birth and burial, family relationships, important people, related settlements and occupied positions. It was conducted on a sample of 250 biographies. The texts were written in Polish from the 1930s onwards and covered characters from various historical periods. The results show that the LLM is very effective in identifying basic personal data, important family relationships, and finding occupations or offices held by the characters. Weaker results were obtained when attempting to find institutions and places associated with protagonists. It suggests that LLMs can significantly assist in digitising and structuring historical biographical data and offer a promising tool for improving historical knowledge bases and speeding up the work compared to manual information extraction.
